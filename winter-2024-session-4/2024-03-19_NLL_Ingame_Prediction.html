Team-Agnostic Model

The first model I used was a team agnostic model. It takes the regulation average goals per hour (goals per game but with overtime removed ), and uses that as the future goal rate for each team in a joint independent poisson model.

The model just makes a matrix of the joint probabilities of each future goals scored up to 30 per team. Then it adds up all the ones that lead to the home team winning, dividing out the ones that lead to a tie.

Incorporating Team Strength

The next model I tried also used Poisson, but instead of treating teams as identical, it estimates how each home team will perform against each away team by estimating individual team effects and a home team effect. It does this with a poisson glm which is a standard practice from the 80s.

mod_glm_home = glm(home_score ~ home_str + away_str, 
                   family=poisson, data=dat_game_ends)

mod_glm_away = glm(away_score ~ home_str + away_str, 
                   family=poisson, data=dat_game_ends)

\[\log(\mu) = \alpha + \sum_i\beta_{home,i} + \sum_j\gamma_{away,j} + \epsilon\]

Calibration

Its not accuracy Im looking for by calibration. I want the 60 percent wins to be right 60 percent of the time. I want the 70 percent wins to be right 70 percent of the time. How do I check that? I group together percentage predictions by 5 percent buckets and see how well they do

Future Work

Theres lots more I could put into a model like this if I had the time and inclination.

  • penalty power play effects
  • injuries to the team roster
  • ahead/behind dynamics (e.g.in the NHL the team thats behind takes more shots, but worse shots)
  • over dispersion (i.e.bursts of goals) in the form of zero inflated poisson, or negative binomial.
  • different scoring rates in different quarters
