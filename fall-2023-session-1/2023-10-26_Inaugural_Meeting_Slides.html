Introduction

What is sports analytics?

Sports analytics is the analysis of data across different domains of sport, including business, performance evaluation, player acquisition, and health and safety.

Why sports analytics?

You’re probably here because you like a sport, or multiple. You have questions about how to leverage data to better understand the inner workings of your sport of interest. Additionally, analyzing sports data is a great way to improve upon your data science, programming, and biomechanics skills! Projects a great resume builder and will beef up your Github, and you can use them as examples of past work during interviews.

How sports analytics? (What analytical software are used?)

Sports analytics was built from the open source community, and as such R, Python, and Julia are the typical languages of choice, augmented by SQL and Tableau.

Project Examples

  • awpy: Package for analyzing CSGO Data
  • Volleydork: Computer vision and volleyball analytics
  • Big Data Derby: Kaggle Competition to analyze horse racing data
  • NWSL Pass Networks: Social network analysis of passes in soccer

Why UWAGGS?

  • Because sports and games are awesome.
  • Because we found there was tons of interest and people working independently.
  • Because rival schools have an analytics group and we don’t.

Sports Analytics Scene at a Glance

  • Almost every professional team has an Analytics or Research and Development department, and this is even permeating its way down to semi-professional and collegiate leagues as well
  • There are over 50 university clubs across North America, including at the University of Toronto, The Queens University, and the University of Western Ontario.
  • There has been a rapid expansion and growth of sports analytics in the public domain, with a growing presence in high quality journals.
  • Even with its growth, the sports analytics space is still very intimate and networking occurs quite naturally. Most people are only a few degrees of separation away, whether that be on a mutual discord server or on social media platforms. The community is very open and friendly!

Inspiration and Timeline of UWAGGS

  • October 2022: David is looking to learn more about sports analytics and signs up with UTSPAN
  • 2 Weeks Later: David finds out that Jack (his Stat 842 professor) is also interested in sports analytics and has industry experience in the area. Jack connects David with the SFU sports analytics group
  • February 2023: David and Jack begin casual conversations about starting a sports analytics club at UWaterloo
  • June 2023: Tim Brecht reaches out to David after the Linköping Hockey Analytics Conference. Tim supports the plan to start up a sports analytics club at UWaterloo and connects David to more students and professors who are also interested in sports analytics
  • September 2023: Tim follows up with David, Jack gives the go-ahead, and David sends an email to the 8 students interested at the time.
  • October 2023: More professors get wind of the club and share it with their students, a one page infographic and signup form is created. 15 students sign up within 48 hours, and 50 have registered by the end of the week.
  • Today: Our first official UWAGGS session, with over 70 declarations of interest

UWAGGS Composition

UWAGGS Sports Interests

Goals for the Club

  1. Form teams and collaborate on projects, hackathons, and competitions
  2. Share resources for tutorials and workshops to enhance our breadth of data science applications to sport
  3. Attend seminars from respected members of the sports analytics community
  4. Present research at and attend conferences

Goal 1: Projects, Hackathons, and Competitions

Examples:

  • Miluwakee Bucks - Analytics Hackathon: Basketball
  • LINHAC: Hockey
  • Big Data Bowl: Football
  • SMT Data Challenge: Baseball
  • French Trot Horse Racing Forecasting Competition: Horse Racing
  • UCONN Sports Analytics Symposium Data Challenge: Gymnastics

Competitions are often divided into categories to promote parity between participants, such as a High School/Undergraduate category and a Graduate Student/Open category. Some take place annually, others more sporadically, but there’s always something going on. Some competitions take place over a weekend in true “hackathon” style, while others last for months. Projects unaffiliated with a competition provide greater freedom and flexibility for exploration and extension.

Goal 2: Resources, Tutorials, and Workshops

Examples:

  • guideR: Sports analytics “bible”, an excellent starting place for those new to this.
  • UTSPAN Hour: Weekly tutorial sessions
  • Sports Dataverse: A suite of R, Python, and Node.js packages for extracting and analyzing sports data
  • CRAN Task View: Sports Analytics: An overview of some prominent sports analytics packages available in R
  • Statsbomb Soccer Data: Free soccer data!

We will host some tutorials of our own as well, but will heavily encourage attending sessions held by other clubs to build connections and not reinvent the wheel.

Goal 3: Seminars

Examples

  • SFU Virtual Seminar Series: Tomorrow at 1pm!
  • Invited Talks

With sufficient interest, the plan is to organize speakers from the sports analytics industry to present their work at different events. There are also plans for a joint Ontario sports analytics showcase hosted by the UTSPAN in Toronto in the Winter semester.

Goal 4: Research

Examples:

  • Carnegie Mellon Sports Analytics Conference
  • SABR Analytics Conference
  • Sport Innovation (SPIN) Summit

These are tailored for students interested in attaining formal publications in journals and conference proceedings, or wanting to attend conferences to grow their knowledge and network. Great opportunity to meet industry professionals and hiring managers!
